Carbonara

Carbonara is made with guanciale (cured pork), eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, spaghetti pasta, and lots of black pepper. Italians don't add extra ingredients like cream, milk, garlic, or onions. Try this recipe if you want to make an authentic, creamy carbonara that comes straight from Italy, where I live. Buon appetito!

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 150g guanciale (or pancetta)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 100g Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt (for pasta water)
  • Fresh parsley (optional)

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  2. Cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente.
  3. In a pan, cook the guanciale until crispy. Remove from heat.
  4. In a bowl, whisk together eggs and grated Pecorino Romano cheese.
  5. Drain the pasta, reserving some pasta water.
  6. Add the hot pasta to the guanciale and mix well.
  7. Remove from heat and quickly add the egg mixture, stirring vigorously to create a creamy sauce. Add reserved pasta water if needed.
  8. Season with black pepper and serve immediately with extra cheese on top.
  9. Garnish with fresh parsley if desired.
  10. Enjoy your authentic Italian carbonara!
